# what does output of i frame command in gdb

when you type "i frame" you are see info about stack frame. almost all data you are understand but stop on most important. See:
```html
(gdb) i frame
Stack level 0, frame at 0x7fffffffd5c0:
 rip = 0x600cb6 in test_work_for_ret (<exploit.c:9); saved rip = 0x4646
 called by frame at 0x7fffffffd5c8
 source language c.
 Arglist at 0x7fffffffd5b0, args: 
 Locals at 0x7fffffffd5b0, Previous frame's sp is 0x7fffffffd5c0
 Saved registers:
  rbp at 0x7fffffffd5b0, rip at 0x7fffffffd5b8
```

now, you will understand sense of all rip's:
- first rip(rip = 0x600cb6) - means how next instruction will be executed
- second rip(saved rip = 0x4646) - means value of return instruction pointer
- third rip(rip at 0x7fffffffd5b8) - means addres of the stack you're taking your time
